Dosa Batter

Intro

NoteThe batter can be used for making idly and dosa. I recomment making idly the first day as idlies do not turn out soft after the batter is refridgerated. Cooked rice can be replaced by rice flakes (poha). Soak poha in water for 5-10 min just before grinding the ingredients and use it instead of cooked rice. Fenugreek seeds will give a lovely golden brown colour to the dosa.

Ingredients

Batter Ingredients
Urad dal3/4 cup
White rice3/4 cup
Idly rice3/4 cup
Fenugreek seeds1/2 tsp
Cooked rice1/2 cup
salt1 1/2 tsp
Water

Steps

  1. Soak urad dal, white rice, idly rice and fenugreek seeds in a container for 8 to 10 hours. Put them all in one container for soaking, no need to seperate them.
  2. Grind the soaked ingredients and cooked rice in a mixer into a fine paste. Add as much water as required for a thick batter. I use around 3/4 to 1 cup of water. The batter would feel granular to touch which is ok. Depending on the size of your mixer container you might have to do multiple batches.
  3. Mix the ground batter with clean hands and then leave it for fermenting which can take from 8 to 24 hours depending on the place you live. In a cold place I recommend leaving the batter in a warm-ish area, near you stove or in the oven with just the oven lights on. In a warm place, leaving the batter on your kitchen counter would do the trick.
  4. Add salt only just before using the batter. After adding salt, refridgerate the remaining batter.
  5. Add more water to dilute the batter if you find it too thick and it is ready to make delicious dosas and idlies.
